I’m Joseph Sciotto, a mechanical engineering student at Columbia University interested in the intersection of mechanical design, simulation, hardware, and software.

My work usually sits between physical systems and computational tools: CAD models, experimental test setups, automation scripts, embedded hardware projects, simulation workflows, and infrastructure projects. I like projects where the final result has to work in the real world, not just look good on paper.

I’m especially interested in:

- Mechanical design and manufacturing
- CAD, simulation, and analysis workflows
- Embedded systems and hardware/software integration
- Engineering tools, automation, and infrastructure
- Aerospace, propulsion, and defense-related systems

Outside of coursework, I enjoy building practical projects that force me to connect theory with implementation. I use this site to document selected projects, explain design tradeoffs, and show the process behind the final result.
